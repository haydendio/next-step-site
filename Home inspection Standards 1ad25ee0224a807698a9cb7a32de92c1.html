Inspection Report & Standards of Practice

Our Inspection Standards

At Next Step Home Inspections, we follow the InterNACHI Home Inspection Standards of Practice, ensuring a thorough, professional, and unbiased assessment of your property. These standards define what is included in our inspections, what limitations exist, and what clients can expect from our reports.

Our inspections provide:

✔ A visual, non-invasive evaluation of all major home components

✔ A detailed report delivered within 24 hours

✔ Clear explanations of any issues found

✔ Recommendations for repairs or further evaluations

What’s Included in Your Home Inspection?

We inspect the following major systems and components in your home:

1. Roof

✔ Inspection of roof-covering materials (shingles, tiles, etc.)

✔ Evaluation of gutters, downspouts, vents, flashing, skylights, chimneys

✔ Identification of active leaks or visible damage

🔹 What’s NOT included:

❌ Walking on roofs that appear unsafe

❌ Testing underground downspout drainage

❌ Warranty or certification of roof lifespan

2. Exterior

✔ Inspection of siding, trim, eaves, soffits, and fascia

✔ Assessment of windows, doors, stairs, railings, and decks

✔ Examination of walkways, driveways, grading, and drainage

🔹 What’s NOT included:

❌ Inspection of outbuildings, fences, and solar panels

❌ Geological assessments or soil stability evaluations

❌ Operation of security systems

3. Basement, Foundation, Crawlspace & Structure

✔ Inspection of visible foundation walls, floors, and structural components

✔ Evaluation of signs of movement, cracks, or moisture intrusion

✔ Checking for wood contact with soil and potential termite risks

🔹 What’s NOT included:

❌ Entry into unsafe crawlspaces

❌ Detailed engineering assessments of foundation strength

❌ Predicting future structural issues

4. Heating & Cooling Systems

✔ Inspection of furnace, heat pump, and air conditioning systems

✔ Verification of proper thermostat function

✔ Checking for visible defects or malfunctions

🔹 What’s NOT included:

❌ Performance testing (BTU calculations, airflow balancing, energy efficiency)

❌ Testing in extreme weather conditions that could damage the system

❌ Inspection of portable heating/cooling units

5. Plumbing System

✔ Inspection of water supply and drain systems

✔ Testing of all faucets, sinks, tubs, and toilets

✔ Checking the water heater, shut-off valves, and sump pumps

🔹 What’s NOT included:

❌ Water quality testing or analysis for contaminants

❌ Inspection of underground or hidden pipes

❌ Operation of shut-off valves

6. Electrical System

✔ Inspection of main service panel and circuit breakers

✔ Checking a representative number of outlets, switches, and light fixtures

✔ Testing of ground-fault circuit interrupters (GFCIs) and smoke detectors

🔹 What’s NOT included:

❌ Inspection of low-voltage systems (internet, phone, alarm systems)

❌ Opening sealed electrical components

❌ Code compliance verification

7. Fireplace & Chimney

✔ Inspection of fireplace structure, lintels, and damper doors

✔ Checking for visible cracks or damage

🔹 What’s NOT included:

❌ Inspection of internal flues or chimney liners

❌ Lighting pilot flames

❌ Testing for proper draft

8. Attic, Insulation & Ventilation

✔ Checking for proper insulation depth and ventilation

✔ Identifying visible signs of moisture or mold

✔ Inspecting bathroom, kitchen, and laundry exhaust vents

🔹 What’s NOT included:

❌ Disturbing or moving insulation materials

❌ Testing air quality or insulation R-values

9. Doors, Windows & Interior

✔ Inspection of doors, windows, walls, ceilings, and floors

✔ Testing of garage door openers and safety sensors

✔ Checking stairs, railings, and handrails for safety compliance

🔹 What’s NOT included:

❌ Evaluation of cosmetic issues (paint, wallpaper, flooring scratches)

❌ Testing of blinds, curtains, or non-permanent fixtures

Limitations, Exceptions & Exclusions

What a Home Inspection Does NOT Cover:

  • Hidden or Concealed Defects – The inspection is visual-only and does not include invasive testing.
  • Code Compliance Checks – We do not verify if the home meets local building codes.
  • Predictions of Future Failures – We assess the current condition, but cannot predict when a component will fail.
  • Environmental Hazards – Inspections do not include mold, asbestos, radon, lead paint, or air quality testing (unless requested separately).
